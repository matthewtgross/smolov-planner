# Smolov Weight Lifting Program Planner

A spreadsheet-based planning tool for the Smolov backsquat program - one of the most intense and effective strength training protocols for increasing your squat 1-rep max (1RM).

## Overview

This Excel spreadsheet (`smolov.mtg.xlsx`) helps you plan and track your entire Smolov squat cycle. It automatically calculates workout dates, weights based on your 1RM, and provides a visual calendar for the 13-week program.

## Quick Start Guide

### Step 1: Set Your Start Date
Open the spreadsheet and locate cell **D3** (labeled "Start Date"). Enter the date you plan to begin the program. The spreadsheet will automatically calculate all subsequent workout dates.

### Step 2: Enter Your Working 1RM
Locate the **"Working 1RM"** field and enter your current estimated 1-rep max for the back squat. This value is used to calculate all working weights for the program.

### Step 3: Review Your Calendar
The spreadsheet generates a day-by-day calendar showing:
- Which phase you're in (Intro, Base, Switching, Intense, Taper)
- Daily workout details
- Rest days

### Step 4: Track Your Progress
As you complete workouts, you can reference the percentage tables to know exactly what weights to use each day.

## Program Structure

The Smolov program consists of **5 phases** over approximately **13 weeks**:

### Phase 1: Introduction Microcycle (1 Week)
A preparatory week to condition your body for the intense training ahead.

| Day | Sets × Reps | % of 1RM |
|-----|-------------|----------|
| Monday | 3 × 8 | 65% |
| Wednesday | 3 × 5 | 70% |
| Friday | 3 × 4 | 75% |
| Saturday | 3 × 3 | 80% |

### Phase 2: Base Microcycle (4 Weeks)
The foundation phase with high-volume squatting to build raw strength.

**Typical schedule:**
- Monday: 4 × 9 @ 70%
- Wednesday: 5 × 7 @ 75%
- Friday: 7 × 5 @ 80%
- Saturday: 10 × 3 @ 85%

*Note: The spreadsheet allows for weight progression throughout this phase.*

### Phase 3: Switching Microcycle (2 Weeks)
A deload/recovery phase where you switch to complementary exercises. This phase is critical for recovery before the intense phase.

### Phase 4: Intense Microcycle (4 Weeks)
The most demanding phase with heavier weights and higher intensity.

| Day | Sets × Reps | % of 1RM |
|-----|-------------|----------|
| Monday | 3 × 3 | 65% / 60% |
| Wednesday | 4 × 4 | 70% / 75% |
| Friday | 5 × 5 | 80% / 85% |
| Saturday | 3 × 3 @ 85% or singles up to 95% |

### Phase 5: Taper Week (1 Week)
A recovery week before testing your new 1RM.

- Monday: 3 × 3 @ 70%
- Wednesday: 4 × 4 @ 75%
- Friday: Light work or rest
- Saturday: Test new 1RM

## Background: The Smolov Methodology

### Origins
The Smolov program was developed by **Sergey Smolov**, a Russian sports scientist and weightlifting coach. It was originally published in the Russian magazine "Hercules" and later translated and popularized in the West, becoming legendary in powerlifting and strength training circles.

### Core Principles

**1. High-Frequency Specialization**
The program focuses exclusively on the back squat, training it 3-4 times per week. This high frequency creates rapid neural adaptations and muscle growth specific to the squat pattern.

**2. Progressive Overload**
Each phase builds upon the previous one, systematically increasing:
- Volume (total reps per session)
- Intensity (% of 1RM)
- Density (work per unit time)

**3. The "Base" Philosophy**
The Base Microcycle creates foundational strength through high-volume work. This phase:
- Builds work capacity
- Develops muscular endurance
- Prepares connective tissue for heavier loads
- Trains mental fortitude

**4. Peaking Strategy**
The Intense Microcycle converts the foundational strength from the Base phase into maximal strength through heavier weights and lower reps, culminating in a new 1RM test.

### Why It Works

1. **Specificity:** Training only squats maximizes neural adaptation to that specific movement
2. **Volume Accumulation:** High volume creates significant hypertrophy and strength gains
3. **Planned Recovery:** The switching phase prevents overtraining before the intense phase
4. **Periodization:** The linear progression with built-in phases prevents plateaus

### Expected Results

The program is known for producing dramatic strength gains:
- **Beginners/Intermediates:** 30-50+ lbs increase in squat 1RM
- **Advanced lifters:** 20-40+ lbs increase
- **Elite lifters:** 10-30+ lbs increase

Results vary based on:
- Training experience
- Recovery capacity
- Nutrition
- Sleep quality
- Previous squat training

## Important Considerations

### Who Should Use Smolov?

**Recommended for:**
- Intermediate to advanced lifters (2+ years training)
- Those with a solid squat technique
- Lifters with a tested 1RM
- Athletes with good recovery capacity
- Those able to dedicate 13 weeks to squat specialization

**NOT recommended for:**
- Beginners (build base strength first)
- Those with knee or hip injuries
- Athletes in-season or with other training priorities
- Anyone unable to commit to high-frequency training

### Prerequisites Before Starting

1. **Establish a true 1RM** - Don't estimate; test it
2. **Ensure technique proficiency** - Poor form under fatigue = injury
3. **Plan recovery** - Sleep 8+ hours, eat sufficiently
4. **Clear your schedule** - This program demands full commitment
5. **Have support equipment** - Belt, knee sleeves, proper footwear

### Common Modifications

- **Smolov Jr.:** A condensed 3-week version for shorter cycles
- **Front Squat Smolov:** Adapted for front squats
- **Powerlifting Meet Prep:** Modified peaking phase for competition
